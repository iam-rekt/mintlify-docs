title: "Make your first RobinSwap trade"
description: "A guided first swap on Robinhood Chain."
---

This tutorial takes you from a connected wallet to a confirmed swap. It uses the default Robinhood Chain route and does not require wrapped ETH in advance.

<Info>
  You need a wallet with Robinhood Chain enabled and enough ETH for network gas plus the token you intend to trade.
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open RobinSwap and connect">
    Go to [robinswap.finance](https://www.robinswap.finance) and choose **Connect wallet**. RobinSwap reads your active wallet address only after you approve the connection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch to Robinhood Chain">
    The swap surface defaults to Robinhood Chain. If your wallet is elsewhere, use the switch prompt. The chain ID is `4663`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose what you are sending">
    Select the **From** token and enter an amount. Native ETH is supported in the interface; RobinSwap handles wrapping only when the selected onchain route requires it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose what you want to receive">
    Select the **To** token. Search the active network’s token list by symbol, name, or contract address. Always verify the address for imported tokens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the live quote">
    RobinSwap updates the quote when token or amount changes and refreshes it periodically. Review the expected output and **Minimum received** before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve, then swap">
    ERC-20 inputs may require a one-time approval. Confirm the approval, then confirm the swap transaction in your wallet. Native ETH does not need an ERC-20 approval.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Check>
  Once the receipt confirms, the output token balance updates in your wallet. Eligible RobinSwap activity can be recorded for weekly points after your one-time points registration signature.
</Check>

## What happened onchain?

For a RobinSwap pool, the router executes against the deployed V2 or V3 contracts. If no local pool can quote, the interface can use a supported routing provider while keeping the experience in one swap card. Read [how routing works](/guides/swap) before trading a route that is not local.
